I remember the first time I met Andrew and Mike. We were on the track, all of three of just finished a run independently of each other. They walked over to me and said "hey, do you want to start a cross country club?" I didn't even hestitate with a joyful "yes".

None of us had made the college D1 program. We were all about 3-4 secounds outside of what we needed to run to run for the official Cal Poly cross country program. However, none of us wanted to give up the sport. We had been running for 4+ years on our own and wanted to continue. That spring, 2010, we started the Cal Poly Distnace club.

Since then, the club has grown to 100 members, racing both on the local and national levels. We're coached by a world class mentor, Mando. As the club has grown, we've seen it shape and develop into a larger entity that we had ever envisioned. Originally, we had just thrown together a quick website template and slapped on the colors. However, that slapstick, throw-away brand couldn't keep up if the CPDC wanted to legitimize itself.

Over the past couple of months, I've had the opportunity to dive back into the roots of the CPDC and translate the emotive infrastructure into a living visual identity that any current and new members can resonate with. Mike, Andrew and I started the club on the idea that anybody should be able to run and compete. It should be an approachable community that helps carry each other over the finish line. I've been pushing the brand to reflect that.

The site is currently underconstruction. I hope to wrap up development by the end of the year before the start of the new track season. This page will reflect the latest news and updates on that.
